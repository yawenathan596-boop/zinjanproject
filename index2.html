About the Park

Murchison Falls National Park is the largest national park in Uganda located in north-western Uganda along the Victoria Nile. The park covers approximately 3,893 square kilometers and stretches from Lake Albert to Karuma Falls.

The park is famous for Murchison Falls where the River Nile forces itself through a narrow 7-meter gorge before plunging 43 meters down creating a spectacular waterfall and rainbow mist.

Bird Watching

The park is a paradise for bird lovers with hundreds of bird species.

Established in 1952, the park is managed by the Uganda Wildlife Authority and is one of the most visited tourist destinations in Uganda.

Wildlife

Mammals

The park hosts over 76 mammal species including lions, elephants, buffaloes, leopards, giraffes, Uganda kobs, waterbucks, warthogs and hyenas.

Primates

Budongo Forest within the park is home to chimpanzees, olive baboons, black-and-white colobus monkeys and blue monkeys.

Bird Species

More than 450 bird species live here including the shoebill stork, African fish eagle, grey crowned crane, kingfishers and goliath herons.

Travel Information

Location: Northwestern Uganda

Distance from Kampala: About 300 km (5-6 hours drive)

Nearest Town: Masindi

Best Time to Visit

The dry seasons (December – February and June – September) are the best times for wildlife viewing because animals gather around water sources.

Park Size

Approximately 3,893 square kilometers.
